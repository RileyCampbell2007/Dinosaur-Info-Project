What is a Dinosaur?

Dinosaurs are a diverse group of reptiles of the clade Dinosauria. They first appeared during the Triassic period, between 243 and 233.23 million years ago (mya), and when extinct about 65 to 66 mya. This group of animals dominated the Earth from the ocean floor to the skies above. There was no major group of organisms that the dinosaurs competed with besides small mammals.

Around 66 million years ago, at the end of the Cretaceous period, an asteroid struck the Earth, triggering a mass extinction that killed off the dinosaurs and some 75% of all species. Somehow mammals survived, thrived, and became dominant across the planet.
